  • Tue 14 July 2015

    • Dr. Jin Zhang, Dr. Dongzhou Zhang, Dr. Ruqing Xu and Dr. Przemek Dera finished the TDS measurement at high-pressure conditions with regular Mar CCD detector at sector 34.

  • Mon 29 June 2015

    • Dr. Jin Zhang received the first version of universal membrane cap, membranes are waiting to be ordered.

  • Tue 21 April 2015

    • Dr. Jin Zhang developed a primitive python code for universal TDS fitting under single-phonon scattering assumption. Results are in good agreement with the original inversion results.

  • Wed 1 April 2015

    • Dr. Jin Zhang worked with Dr. Ruqing Xu developed the first version of python code for Si single crystal TDS fitting. Results are in good agreement with previous measurements.

  • Thu 5 March 2015

    • Dr. Jin Zhang, Dr. Dongzhou Zhang, Dr. Ruqing Xu and Dr. Przemek Dera performed the first high-pressure TDS measurement on Si single crystals at 13BMC in GSECARS with the help from beam line scientists Dr. Peter Eng and Dr. Joanne Stubbs, using the flight path + Pilatus detector setup. Dr. Jin Zhang and Dr. Dongzhou Zhang worked out the method and code for calibrating the detector pixel - crystal hkl relation.

  • Fri 20 February 2015

    • Dr. Jin Zhang received the first set of modified W-Al2O3 heaters.

  • Thu 29 January 2015

    • Dr. Jin Zhang performed primitive test on W-Al2O3 heaters for diamond anvil cell resistive heating, came up with modified designs for heaters.

  • Fri 9 January 2015

    • Dr. Jin Zhang finished the design of the first version universal membrane cap, which is a single-membrane DAC cap ideal for precise remote pressure control during compressional process. It fits most DACs people used in high-pressure community without losing angular opening, including Princeton type and Japanese piston cylinder DAC, modified 3-pin and 4-pin Merrill-Bassett-type DACs, BX90 DAC, etc.

  • Sun 14 December 2014

    • Initial collaboration with Dr. Ruqing Xu on TDS measurement at high-pressure conditions. Tests were made under ambient conditions on thin (~15 microns) samples comparable to DAC samples.
